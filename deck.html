The Problem

Tangible assets are the most contested part of any estate — and the most invisible.

Estate planning has been built entirely around financial assets: bank accounts, real estate, investments. But research consistently shows that families fight most about the things money can't replace — and there's no infrastructure for any of it.

The Three Gaps

Why this problem has three distinct layers — and why fixing one isn't enough.

The problem isn't a single failure point. It's three compounding gaps that existing products each fail to close. Heirlo is the first product designed to address all three simultaneously.

Gap 01
📄

The Documentation Gap

Most people never document physical asset intent at all. 76% of Americans have no will — meaning their jewelry, collections, pets, and heirlooms have no legal assignment whatsoever. These assets default to state intestacy law, which doesn't know about grandmother's ring or who really wanted the dog.

76%
of Americans have zero estate documentation. The default for their physical belongings is court-supervised chaos.
Gap 02
📋

The Specificity Gap

Even among the 24% with wills, physical belongings are described as "personal property to be divided among heirs." This is not an assignment — it is an instruction to argue. The legal instrument exists but provides no item-level clarity. The will that says "my jewelry to my children" has just created a dispute between three siblings.

15%
of heirs name personal item distribution as the #1 conflict source in estate matters — vs. only 3% for financial assets.
Gap 03
🤝

The Acknowledgment Gap

Even a perfect personal property list is discovered in a filing cabinet after a funeral, by people in grief, with no way to ask clarifying questions. Recipients never confirmed they wanted the item. They never knew it was coming. Nobody heard the reason behind the decision. Heirlo is the only product that closes this loop — while the owner is still alive.

52%
of people don't know where their parents stored estate planning documents. Heirlo makes the document irrelevant — because the intent is already confirmed.
Why This Matters for the Pitch
When investors ask "can't people just list things in a will?" — the three-gap framework is your answer. A will helps with Gap 1 (sometimes). It does nothing for Gap 2 (item-level specificity) and makes Gap 3 worse (discovered after death, no acknowledgment, no recipient confirmation). Heirlo is the only product designed to close all three.

Legal Positioning

Heirlo is not a legal service. It is a documentation layer that makes legal services work better.

The single most important legal distinction for investors, users, and regulators: Heirlo does not practice law. It produces a legally recognized instrument — the personal property memorandum — that exists precisely because legislatures designed it to be created without an attorney. This distinction is the foundation of Heirlo's business model, its regulatory moat, and its scalability.

The Instrument
The Personal Property Memorandum — designed by law to exist without a lawyer.
Under the Uniform Probate Code (UPC) — adopted in whole or in part by the majority of U.S. states — a testator may incorporate by reference a separate written list that directs the disposition of tangible personal property. This instrument, known as a Personal Property Memorandum (PPM), is legally binding when it is: (1) signed by the testator, (2) dated, and (3) either referred to in a will or filed independently as a notarized declaration of intent.

Heirlo generates exactly this document. It does not draft wills. It does not give legal advice. It produces a standardized, notarized PPM that sits alongside — and strengthens — any existing or future will. Heirlo is not a legal service. It is the infrastructure that makes this legal instrument accessible for the first time at consumer scale.

Heirlo Is
✅
A documentation platform. Heirlo creates, stores, and notarizes personal property memoranda — a specific, legally defined instrument that does not require attorney involvement.
✅
A notarization facilitator. Heirlo connects users with licensed notaries through a remote online notarization (RON) workflow, which is legally recognized in 42+ states.
✅
An attorney referral platform. Heirlo's marketplace connects users to licensed estate attorneys for services that go beyond what the PPM covers — wills, trusts, guardianship. This is an existing, well-established model (LegalZoom, Nolo, Avvo).
✅
A data custodian. Heirlo stores user asset data, assignment intent, and acknowledgment records — with explicit user consent, SOC 2-aligned infrastructure, and clear data portability rights.
Heirlo Is Not
❌
A law firm. Heirlo does not provide legal advice, draft wills or trusts, represent users in any legal proceeding, or make representations about the enforceability of any specific assignment under any specific jurisdiction's law.
❌
A will replacement. A personal property memorandum governs tangible personal property only. It does not replace a will, does not govern real estate, financial accounts, or digital assets, and does not supersede beneficiary designations.
❌
A fiduciary. Heirlo has no duty of care over the assets themselves, no role in administering an estate, and no authority over the actual transfer of property at the time of death. That role belongs to executors, probate courts, and attorneys.
❌
A competitor to attorneys. Heirlo is the pre-legal layer that creates documented intent before anyone needs a lawyer. Every Heirlo user who eventually needs estate counsel is a warm referral — not a lost customer.

Why "not a legal service" is the product's biggest strength

Most founders treat legal positioning as a liability to manage. For Heirlo, it is the source of scale. Here's why.

For Users — Lower Barrier, Higher Adoption
🚪
No attorney required to start. The #1 reason 76% of Americans have no estate planning is the friction of the legal process. Heirlo removes that friction entirely — the PPM is designed by statute to be user-generated.
📱
Mobile-native behavior, not a legal event. When people don't perceive they're "doing legal work," adoption rates are orders of magnitude higher. Heirlo is positioned as a documentation behavior — like a photo album with legal weight.
🔄
Updatable without cost or friction. Traditional legal documents require attorney involvement to amend. Heirlo users can update assignments in seconds, which creates ongoing engagement and recurring document generation revenue.
For Investors — Regulatory Clarity, Not Risk
🛡️
Not subject to unauthorized practice of law (UPL) regulation. Heirlo produces a standardized document, not legal advice. This is the same model used by LegalZoom, Rocket Lawyer, and Trust & Will — all of which have scaled to hundreds of millions in revenue without law firm licensing.
📈
The attorney marketplace is upside, not a workaround. Heirlo's legal positioning turns jurisdiction complexity into a revenue stream — partial/non-UPC states trigger premium referrals, not product failure.
🤝
Attorneys are distribution partners, not competitors. Estate attorneys who recommend Heirlo to clients get more organized, higher-intent clients. The referral relationship flows both ways — Heirlo to attorneys, and attorneys back into Heirlo's B2B licensing layer.

The regulatory moat

Heirlo's legal positioning isn't just defensible — it creates durable competitive advantages that grow harder to replicate over time.

Moat 01 · Statutory Basis
The instrument exists in law. Heirlo just deploys it.
The personal property memorandum has been part of the Uniform Probate Code since 1969. It is not a Heirlo invention — it is an underutilized statutory instrument that Heirlo is first to bring to consumer scale. Competitors can build a better app; they cannot change the law that makes the instrument valid.
UPC §2-513 · Adopted 1969 · ~56 years of legal precedent Heirlo deploys at $9.99
Moat 02 · Notarization Network
RON infrastructure compounds with scale.
Heirlo's remote online notarization (RON) partnerships — enabled by legislation in 42+ states — become more valuable with volume. As Heirlo notarizes more documents, it builds a notarization cost advantage, a quality signal to courts, and a proprietary timestamp record that competitors cannot retroactively replicate. The notarization layer is a trust moat.
RON legally recognized in 42+ states · Grows in value with every document executed
Moat 03 · Acknowledgment Record
Heirlo holds the only pre-death consent record in existence.
No court, no attorney, and no other product maintains a timestamped, digitally witnessed record of a recipient's pre-death acknowledgment of a specific asset assignment. When a Heirlo assignment is contested in probate, Heirlo's acknowledgment record is the strongest possible evidence of unambiguous intent — and it makes Heirlo the only platform courts will want to reference.
No comparable record exists anywhere · Creates evidentiary value that compounds with every assignment

Investor Objections

The hardest questions — and how to answer them with data.

These are the problem and market objections most likely to come up in diligence. Each one has a sourced, specific answer that reframes the question as a strength.

"Personal property disputes are a small part of estate conflicts — most fights are about money."
The Allianz/Age Wave data says the opposite: emotional possessions are 5× more likely to cause disputes than financial assets, and 15% of heirs cite personal item distribution as the #1 source of conflict vs. 3% for financial assets. This is counterintuitive — and that's what makes it a strong category. The category is real and underserved precisely because it doesn't look obvious from the outside.
"131M households is a fake TAM — most of them would never pay for this."
The SOM is the honest number: $48M+ ARR from converting 1% of pet-owning households at $120 ARPU. That's the wedge, not the vision. The vision is 131M households. The believable near-term story is 400K users by Year 3, generating $13.5M ARR across three revenue layers. Use the SOM to anchor credibility; use the TAM to anchor ambition.
"Can't people just write a personal property memorandum with their attorney?"
They can — but it costs hundreds of dollars, requires a lawyer visit, isn't photo-based, isn't mobile, is expensive to update, and isn't acknowledged by recipients before death. 76% of Americans don't have a will at all. The personal property memorandum is not a consumer product — it's a professional service that most people never access. Heirlo makes it a five-minute mobile behavior.
"Why is will ownership declining? Doesn't that mean people don't care about estate planning?"
Declining will ownership confirms that formal legal products have a friction problem, not that people don't care. 20% of people without a will have already talked to a loved one about their wishes — they just never formalized it. 16% have started online research. The desire is real; the barrier is the process. Heirlo removes the barrier with a mobile-first, behavior-first approach that doesn't start with a lawyer.
"Doesn't Heirlo just create family conflict by surfacing intent while people are still alive?"
Heirlo moves conflict from the worst possible moment (a funeral, with grief, no owner to clarify) to the most manageable one (while the owner is alive and can explain, update, and resolve). The heirlo is a living document by design — nothing is binding until the owner chooses to notarize. That flexibility is what makes people willing to use it. Same principle as a will: you can update anytime, and the ability to update is why people commit.
