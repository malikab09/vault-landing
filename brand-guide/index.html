01 · The mark · three styles
One dandelion,
three readings.
Geometric for product. Refined for marketing. Engraved for documents. Same metaphor — seeds drifting from a centre — drawn three ways for three contexts. Each style ships in a variant for light surfaces and a variant for dark.
01 · Geometric
For the product.
Eight rays at forty-five degree intervals. Tipped seed dots. Single weight. Built for system clarity.

02 · Refined
For the brand.
Sixty-six brass tufts. Hand-positioned, varying in size and distance. Drift seeds escape the field. The marketing reading — distinctive, dimensional.

03 · Engraved
For documents.
Multi-line cross-hatch. Hairline strokes. The legal-document reading — formal, archival, fine-print.

24-line cross-hatch
Naming convention. Bare filename = dark stroke for light surfaces (e.g. seed-geometric.svg). The -light suffix = light stroke for dark surfaces (e.g. seed-geometric-light.svg renders cream #F2E9DC for cinematic / dark backgrounds). The refined style additionally has a -brass variant for brass-on-brass treatments.

05 · Scale & cutoff
The mark
at every size.
From hero to favicon. The full geometric construction holds at 40px and above. Below that, switch to the simplified six-ray reading for legibility. Never let any mark fall below 16px.
96px · full
64px · full
40px · full
24px · 6-ray
16px · 6-ray
Cutoff rule. At 32px and below, switch from the full 12-ray construction to the 6-ray minimal. Stroke-width scales inversely — at 16px use 4px stroke; at 96px use 1.4px. The mark should always feel drawn, not printed.

Browser tab,
at home.
Two purpose-built favicons with rounded square Ink ground and Brass-warm mark. Each is hand-tuned at its native size — the 32px uses six rays, the 16px uses an eight-ray "X-cross" construction so the mark survives sub-pixel rendering.

16px · actual
native size
The smallest legal mark.
Why two constructions. A 32px favicon has enough pixel real estate to render six clean rays; below that, sub-pixel anti-aliasing destroys subtle stroke widths. The 16px version uses thicker strokes and an 8-ray cross pattern (cardinal + diagonal) that survives at any rasterization. Don't substitute one for the other.

08 · Scatter pattern · loose
A breeze passed

A 1600×900 master with seeds at random rotations, sizes, and opacities. For hero backgrounds, video frames, social card edges, the inside of a fold-out brochure. Looks composed; isn't.
Loose · light
Loose · dark
When to use which. Tile = controlled, repeating, structural — works at any container size. Loose = atmospheric, single-use, finite — use as the actual asset, don't tile it. Both ship in light (Linen #F5EFE8 ground) and dark (Ink-deep #14100D ground).

11b · Video
How Heirlo
moves.
Real objects. Natural light. Held shots. Silence as score. Heirlo videos read as documentary, not advertising — the camera is the witness, not the salesperson. These rules govern every film, social cut, and product video.
Opening · closing logo card
Mark above wordmark, centered. Hold for 2.4 seconds. Fade in over 0.6s, hold 1.2s, fade out 0.6s.
asset · dandelion-refined-light.svg
ground · Ink-deep #14100D
wordmark · Fraunces 900 · brass-warm
Lower-third
Bottom-left, 64px from edge. Mark + name + role. Cream ink-soft on a 30% Ink-deep gradient that fades to transparent over 200px from the bottom.
asset · seed-refined-light.svg · 24px
name · Fraunces 300 · 28px
role · DM Sans 500 · 11px · 0.18em
Aspect ratio
cinema · 2.35:1
landscape · 16:9
portrait · 9:16 · 4:5
square · 1:1
Frame at 2.35:1 for primary masters. Crop down for socials. Never stretch.
Color grade
Lift shadows toward Coffee. Warm whites toward Linen. Desaturate cyans. Push amber in highlights — never orange.
shadows · +8 warm
highlights · +12 amber
Sound
Diegetic only. The kettle whistles. The drawer slides. The page turns. No music swells. No scored cues. Silence between actions is the score.
Subject & framing
Frame on objects, not faces. Hands holding the necklace. The recipe card on the counter. The dog's collar in the morning light. When people appear, frame them looking at the object — not at the camera.
Pacing
Hold every shot longer than feels comfortable. Cut on completed actions, not mid-motion. Average shot length should run 4-6 seconds — twice the contemporary documentary norm.
Type on screen. Use Fraunces 300 italic for any spoken-word quote ("She always said that ring was yours"). Use DM Sans 500 for factual identification (names, dates, places). Never type animation — type fades in, holds, fades out. Centered or left-aligned only, never right-aligned.
The film series asset paths. Master files live in /films/heirlo/{number}-{title}/. Each film ships with: a 2.35:1 master, a 16:9 social cut, a 9:16 portrait cut for stories, a 1:1 square cut for feed, and a 6-second teaser for ads. The closing card asset is the same across all five exports — the dandelion holds its proportions regardless of crop.

How Heirlo
shows up.
Heirlo on Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, and X. Less a feed than a quiet field. The brand is recognisable in the grid before any caption is read — because every post is a held image of a real object, not a graphic with text on it.

Grid principle
The grid is the ad. Three rows of nine posts should read as a single mood — warm light, real objects, held moments. Never alternate logo posts and photo posts. The mark appears in stories and bio, not the grid.
Post composition
Single image, 4:5 portrait. Object centered or slightly off-axis. No text overlays. No filters beyond the canonical color grade. The caption does the talking.
Caption length
Instagram · 1–3 sentences
X · 1 sentence, ≤120 chars
LinkedIn · 2–4 sentences
TikTok · caption = title
Specific over general. "My grandmother's recipe card." beats "The things we leave behind."
Hashtags
None on Instagram. One on TikTok if essential (#heirlo). Hashtags read as marketing — Heirlo doesn't market, it documents.
Stories
Vertical 9:16. Mark in the top-left at 32px. Single object per story. Use the dark scatter pattern as a transition between story sets.
Post types · in rotation
The Object · a single item with a one-sentence story. The grandmother's ring. The father's watch. The dog's collar.

The Recipe Card · handwriting close-ups. Always real, never stylized.

The Hands · holding, photographing, handing-over moments. The action of documentation.

The Quiet Quote · Fraunces 300 italic on Linen, sparingly. Never more than one a week.
Never post
— Stock photography of "families."
— Product screenshots without context.
— Logo on a colored background.
— "Day in the life" content.
— Memes, trends, audio-driven content.
— Founder selfies.
— "Tag a friend" prompts.
— Discounts, urgency, scarcity language.
Posting cadence. Three feed posts per week, maximum. Two stories per week. One LinkedIn post per week. Less, but real. If there's nothing real to show, post nothing.

How Heirlo
presents.
Investor pitches, advisor onboarding, partner conversations. Heirlo decks read like editorial — generous white space, one idea per slide, the mark in the same place every time. Never a corporate deck. Never a sales deck. A document shown one page at a time.

Slide proportions

margin · 88px each side

Color rhythm
80% of slides on Linen. 15% on Cream (alternating sections). 5% on Ink-deep. Ink-deep slides are the moments that matter — the section dividers, the closing slide, the single number that defines the company.
Slide count discipline
Pitch deck · 12-15 slides max. Advisor onboarding · 8 slides. Internal briefing · whatever it needs. When in doubt, cut a slide. The deck is not the conversation; the conversation is the conversation.
Animation
None. No fades, no builds, no transitions. Slide-cut only. The rhythm is in the cuts, not the motion. (The exception: the dandelion mark on the closing slide may use the canonical drift animation if delivered as video.)
Image use in decks. Photos must follow the same rules as social: real objects, natural light, no stock. Bleed images full-width across the slide; never crop into rounded corners; never frame a photo with a colored border. Captions in DM Sans 500 11px tracked 0.18em, placed bottom-left of the image.
Master file. Build decks in Keynote (preferred) or Figma slides. Use the canonical PPTX export only when handing off to a partner who needs PowerPoint. Avoid Google Slides for external decks — the rendering doesn't preserve Fraunces, and any deck shipping to investors should look the way Heirlo intends, not how Google interprets it.
12 · Do / Don't
The rules,
plainly stated.
Read these before any new surface. They keep the brand from drifting.
Always
✓Use Fraunces 900 only for the canonical wordmark. The avatar wordmark is the only exception.
✓Pick the correct mark style for context: geometric for product, refined for marketing, engraved for documents.
✓Lead with Linen on light surfaces; reserve Ink-deep gradient for moments of weight.
✓Name specific items, specific people. Generic = invisible.
✓Use natural light and real objects in photography.
✓Hold shots longer than feels comfortable. Silence is the score.
Never
✗Invent a fourth mark style. The three are the three.
✗Use purple, blue, teal, or green anywhere in a Heirlo touchpoint.
✗Use stock photography, staged scenes, or anything that looks like an ad.
✗Distort, rotate, or recolour the wordmark or any dandelion mark.
✗Tile the loose-scatter pattern. It is a finite asset, not a repeat.
✗Say "innovative", "solution", "platform", "users", or "streamline".
